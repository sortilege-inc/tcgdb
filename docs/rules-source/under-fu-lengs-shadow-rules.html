Overview

Under Fu Leng's Shadow is an expansion for Legend of the Five Rings: The Card Game. It introduces new cards, rules, and game components to support playing the game against the Shadowlands faction. The faction is designed to be played as a cooperative experience in which 1–4 players work together to withstand waves of attacks. Alternatively, it can be played as a player-driven challenge deck where one player commands the Shadowlands against a team of 2–4 opponents.

The expansion includes 138 non-Shadowlands cards. These are highlighted in the new formats but can also be used in standard Legend of the Five Rings play.

Two Formats supported

The rules accommodate both formats and assume players already know the normal rules of the game:

  • Stronghold format — uses 5 provinces and a stronghold.
  • Skirmish format — uses 3 provinces (no stronghold).

The rulebook recommends new players learn the skirmish format before the stronghold format. (Learn-to-Play documents for both are at www.L5R.com.)

Components (rules-relevant)

The component list is for identification only; no rules. Counts retained for reference (they bound deck-construction): 3 Shadowlands Warlord cards (double-sided), 10 Shadowlands Province cards, 2 Stronghold cards, 60 Shadowlands Dynasty cards, 60 Shadowlands Conflict cards, 64 (non-Shadowlands) Dynasty cards, 72 (non-Shadowlands) Conflict cards, and 20 double-sided Status tokens (Tainted).

Shadowlands Faction Rules

The Shadowlands faction changes how the game is played: it exists as an external threat that the clans of Rokugan must defeat together.

With the exception of the rules listed below, the Shadowlands faction is treated as a clan just like the seven clans currently in the game. It is identified by the [shadowlands] clan symbol in text and by its unique card frame. The following rules apply to the Shadowlands faction in all game modes.

Team Play

  • The Shadowlands is not meant to be faced by a single opponent. Players who oppose the Shadowlands do so as a team, and the Shadowlands faction operates as a team of one.
  • Even when playing a single-player cooperative game against a Shadowlands deck, all of the rules for team play must be observed.
  • Throughout the rulebook, both the team of players opposing the Shadowlands and the Shadowlands itself are referred to as teams, regardless of the number of players on that team.

Full team rules: Appendix III (p. 24).

Winning the Game (vs. the Shadowlands)

When playing with or against the Shadowlands, only use the following win conditions (the normal honor-total and standard victory conditions do not apply to the Shadowlands side — see Honor below):

  • If the Shadowlands dynasty deck has 0 cards in it at the end of any game round, the Shadowlands deck (or player) loses the game.
  • If all of the Shadowlands provinces are broken, the Shadowlands deck (or player) immediately loses the game.
  • If each player's stronghold province is broken (stronghold format), or all non-Shadowlands provinces are broken (skirmish format), the Shadowlands deck (or player) immediately wins the game.

Honor

  • The Shadowlands faction has no clan honor, and a team of opponents cannot win by accumulating a sufficient honor total.
  • If an effect would cause the Shadowlands deck (or player) to gain or lose honor, it is ignored. Honor costs are always considered to be paid by the Shadowlands.
  • If a player would be instructed to take honor from / give honor to their Shadowlands opponent (or vice-versa), that player gains honor from or loses honor to the general token pool instead.

Surviving the Shadowlands

Because the Shadowlands dynasty deck is also the timer leading toward its opponents' victory condition, card effects cannot discard cards from the Shadowlands dynasty deck.

Banned Cards (when playing against the Shadowlands)

The following cards are banned; players cannot include them in their decks:

  • Trading on the Sand Road (Warriors of the Wind, 28)
  • Haughty Magistrate (Imperial Cycle, 69)
  • Waning Hostilities (Imperial Cycle, 100)
  • Hige's Sermon (Clan War, 92)
  • A Season of War (Dominion Cycle, 14)

Warlords

  • Each Shadowlands deck is built around a unique warlord who defines how the game is played from round to round.
  • Warlord cards are double-sided: one side for cooperative play and one for challenge play. Use the side matching the game mode; the mode is indicated by a tag at the bottom of the card's text box.
  • The warlord starts the game out of play, visible between the two Shadowlands decks.
  • Instead of a cost, the warlord has a fate value in its upper-left corner that indicates how much fate the Shadowlands collects each round.
  • Each warlord has a powerful ability that can be triggered once per round, even while the warlord is out of play.
  • The warlord can be brought into play by card effects, at which point it functions like any other character. When it would leave play, it returns to its out-of-play zone (where its ability can continue to be used and from which it can be brought back into play later).

Example warlord shown on this page (card data, not a rule): Akuma no Oni — "Champion. Corrupted. No [void] attachments." with a once-per-round Reaction, tagged Cooperative. Full warlord card text belongs in the card catalog.

Per-Player Icon

  • The per-player icon [per-player] is used on Shadowlands warlord and province cards to scale a value by the number of players.
  • When a number is followed by the [per-player] symbol, multiply that number by the number of non-Shadowlands players in the game to determine that value.
  • Players will not be eliminated during the game.

Tainted Status and Corrupted Keyword

  • Many Shadowlands card effects can taint a character or province. To indicate a card is tainted, place a tainted status token on it.
  • A tainted card is stronger when tainted, but carries an honor cost in order to attack or defend.
  • Because the Shadowlands is always considered to pay honor costs, the cost to use a tainted card is ignored by the Shadowlands faction.
  • Many Shadowlands characters have the corrupted keyword, which means they enter play already tainted.

Full rules on tainted status and the corrupted keyword: Appendix I (p. 21).

Shadowlands Ring Effects

When a Shadowlands player would resolve a ring effect, they use a different set of ring effects than their opponents (the standard ring effects are corrupted by Jigoku):

  • Air: Each opponent loses 1 honor.
  • Earth: Each opponent chooses 1 faceup card in their provinces and discards it, then discards 1 random card from their hand.
  • Fire: Each opponent chooses and taints a character they control.
  • Water: Each opponent chooses and bows a character in their home.
  • Void: Each opponent removes 1 fate from a character they control.

Assembling Shadowlands Decks

  • Each Shadowlands deck is built around its warlord and contains two 40-card decks (dynasty + conflict) and five provinces.
  • The Shadowlands dynasty and conflict cards are organized into twelve encounter groups of ten cards each, identified by a number on the bottom of each card. There are six dynasty encounter groups and six conflict encounter groups.
  • To build a Shadowlands deck: select the warlord and each of that warlord's provinces (listed by title below). Then select the four 10-card encounter groups listed for the dynasty deck and shuffle them together, and the four listed for the conflict deck and shuffle them together. This creates a 40-card dynasty deck and a 40-card conflict deck. The two decks, five provinces, and warlord together form the Shadowlands deck.
  • For a first game against the Shadowlands, the rulebook recommends playing against Akuma no Oni.
